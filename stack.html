Stack Academy

Understand systems.
Then exploit them.

Stack Academy launches Winter 2027. The curriculum is designed to build deep technical understanding before introducing offensive techniques. Students move through structured learning paths at a measured pace — each level reinforces the one before it.

Taught by experienced practitioners. Delivered online and through intensive in-person bootcamps in Kuwait, using Stack's own private lab infrastructure. Cohorts are kept small to allow meaningful instructor interaction.

Featured Bootcamp

0-Day Research in the
Modern Security World

6 Weeks Advanced Level In-Person · Kuwait

This bootcamp focuses on understanding how software systems fail before discussing how to find those failures. Participants learn memory management, modern exploit mitigations, and vulnerability discovery methodology as practiced in real-world security research.

The curriculum is inspired by the workflows used in professional vulnerability research — including reverse engineering, root cause analysis, and responsible disclosure practices.

This bootcamp does not guarantee that participants will discover vulnerabilities. It provides training in the foundational skills and research methodology used in the field.

Week 1–2
Memory management, stack and heap internals, modern exploit mitigations — ASLR, DEP, CFG, and how they interact.
Week 3
Static and dynamic reverse engineering. Understanding compiled code, debugging workflows, and binary analysis tooling.
Week 4
Root cause analysis methodology. Tracing a bug from symptom to source across real-world vulnerability case studies.
Week 5
Vulnerability discovery workflows. Fuzzing, code auditing, and structured approaches to finding security-relevant conditions.
Week 6
Responsible disclosure: writing clear reports, coordinating with vendors, and understanding the ethics and legal context of security research.
Throughout
Lab sessions using Stack's private infrastructure. Small groups. Direct instructor access throughout all six weeks.

Educational Philosophy

How we think
about learning.

Stack's curriculum is built around a single conviction: practitioners who understand what they are doing produce better work and make better decisions than those who have only learned to execute steps.

01
Understand before exploiting.

Every attack technique has a mechanical explanation. We teach the mechanism first. Students who understand why something works can adapt when conditions change.

02
Learn systems, not tools.

Tools come and go. The underlying system concepts — memory, protocols, file formats, operating system internals — remain. We teach the substrate.

03
Depth over shortcuts.

Progress through the curriculum requires demonstrating understanding, not completing a checklist. We move slowly when necessary.

04
Research before automation.

Automation is a productivity multiplier for people who already understand what they are automating. We do not teach tools as a substitute for that understanding.

05
Build professionals.

The goal is practitioners who can work independently on hard problems — not certification collectors or tool operators. Credentials are a byproduct of competence, not a substitute for it.

06
Ethics is not optional.

Responsible disclosure, legal boundaries, and professional conduct are taught throughout the curriculum — not as a module at the end, but as a baseline requirement from day one.
